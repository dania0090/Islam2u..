Understanding Intersex in Islam

Our website, "Understanding Intersex in Islam," is dedicated to providing comprehensive information, support, and resources about intersex individuals, known as "khunsa" in Islamic terminology. Intersex individuals are born with both male and female characteristics, a phenomenon acknowledged in Islamic teachings. Despite societal reluctance to discuss intersex issues due to concerns about dignity, shame, and emotions, it is essential to recognize that intersex individuals are Allah's creations, deserving of His blessings and sustenance.

Khunsa Musykil

A person with ambiguous signs of masculinity or femininity, possessing both male and female genitalia, making it unclear whether they are male or female.

Khunsa Ghayru Musykil

A person with clear signs of masculinity or femininity, identifiable as male or female despite having an additional sexual organ. Their legal status in matters such as inheritance follows the clear signs of their gender.
